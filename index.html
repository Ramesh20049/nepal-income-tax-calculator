💡 Tax Saving Suggestions
  • 🏦
    Provident Fund (PF): Contributions to employee provident fund are deductible. Maximise your contribution — up to the statutory limit — to reduce taxable income.
  • 📈
    Citizen Investment Trust (CIT): Invest in CIT funds. Contributions are eligible for deduction, lowering your taxable base while building a long-term corpus.
  • 🛡️
    Life Insurance Premium: Premiums paid on life insurance policies are deductible. Ensure your policy is with an IRD-recognised insurer.
  • 🏛️
    Approved Retirement Fund: Contributions to IRD-approved retirement schemes are deductible. Check with your employer if such a scheme is available.
  • 📅
    TDS Timing: If you join mid-year, your TDS is spread over remaining months. Confirm the TDS schedule with your employer's accounts team to avoid year-end surprises.
📖 How Nepal's Progressive Tax System Works

Nepal uses a progressive tax system. This means each portion of your income is taxed at a different rate — not your entire income at the highest rate you fall into.

Think of it as filling buckets. The first Rs. 10 lakh fills the 1% bucket. Once that's full, the next Rs. 5 lakh fills the 10% bucket, and so on. You only pay a higher rate on the extra income above each threshold.

Example: Taxable Income = Rs. 30,00,000 First Rs. 10,00,000 × 1% = Rs. 10,000 Next Rs. 5,00,000 × 10% = Rs. 50,000 Next Rs. 10,00,000 × 20% = Rs. 2,00,000 Next Rs. 5,00,000 × 27% = Rs. 1,35,000 ─────────── Total Tax = Rs. 3,95,000 Effective Rate = 13.17%

Even though Rs. 30 lakh falls in the 27% slab, your effective tax rate is only 13.17% — because most of your income was taxed at lower rates.
